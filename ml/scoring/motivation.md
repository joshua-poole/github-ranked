We analyze the last 90 days of commit activity to calculate an ELO delta (0-5000) that reflects development health. Each commit is scored on five weighted factors: lines changed (35%), message quality via ML sentiment (25%), consistency of pacing (15%), time of day (15%), and weekday vs weekend (10%). Higher scores indicate sustainable patterns—clear communication, regular hours, steady pace—while lower scores flag potential burnout risks like late nights, vague messages, or erratic commits. The goal isn't to judge productivity but to encourage awareness of coding habits that lead to long-term wellbeing.
